### 3. Curriculum & Course Structure (B.Tech Core Subjects)
The B.Tech curriculum follows a structured pattern: a common Institute Core for the first two years, followed by Departmental Compulsory courses.

*   **Computer Science and Engineering (CSE):** Operating Systems (CS330), Principles of Database Systems (CS315), Data Structures & Algorithms (ESO207), Computer Organization (CS220), Theory of Computation (CS340), Compiler Design (CS335), Computer Networks (CS425).
*   **Electrical Engineering (EE):** Introduction to VLSI Design (EE417), MOS Transistor Theory, CMOS Logic Design, Microelectronics, Signals and Systems, Control Systems, Power Electronics.
*   **Mechanical Engineering (ME):** Fluid Mechanics (ME231), Heat and Mass Transfer (ME341), Thermodynamics, Mechanics of Solids (ME251), Theory of Mechanisms and Machines (ME352), Design of Machine Elements (ME351), Manufacturing Science and Technology (ME361).
*   **Aerospace Engineering (AE):** Introduction to Aerospace Engineering (AE201), Compressible Aerodynamics (AE311), Aerospace Structures (AE232/334), Aerospace Propulsion (AE341), Flight Mechanics (AE321).
*   **Chemical Engineering (CHE):** Heat Transfer (CHE212), Mass Transfer & Separation (CHE213), Chemical Reaction Engineering (CHE331), Transport Phenomena (CHE311), Process Dynamics & Control (CHE381).
*   **Civil Engineering (CE):** Structural Analysis (CE222), Soil Mechanics (CE351), Foundation Design (CE352), Environmental Quality & Pollution (CE211), Transportation Engineering (CE371), Hydrology (CE361).
*   **Biological Sciences & Bioengineering (BSBE):** Organ System Physiology & Anatomy (BSE211A), Molecular Cell Biology (BSE311A), Biochemical Engineering (BSE312A), Bioinformatics & Computational Biology (BSE322A), Biomaterials (BSE411A).
*   **Materials Science and Engineering (MSE):** Thermodynamics & Phase Equilibria (MSE201), Phase Transformations (MSE301), Mechanical Behavior of Materials (MSE302), Electronic & Magnetic Properties (MSE303), Principles of Materials Processing (MSE304).

### 4. Campus Life & Infrastructure
**Sports Facilities (Managed by Games and Sports Council):**
- **Main Stadium:** 400m synthetic track, cricket ground, and field event arenas.
- **Aquatics:** Olympic-size 8-lane swimming pool with professional coaching.
- **Indoor Courts:** Wooden-floored Badminton, Table Tennis, and Squash courts (New & Old SAC).
- **Outdoor Courts:** Synthetic Lawn Tennis courts, Basketball courts, and Volleyball courts.
- **Specialized Arenas:** Wall-climbing facility, Skating arena, Football ground, and Hockey ground.

**Active Clubs & Hobby Groups:**
- **Technical (SnT Council):** Aeromodelling Club, Robotics Club, Programming Club (P-Club), Electronics Club, Astronomy Club, Business Club, Finance and Analytics Club.
- **Cultural (MnC Council):** Music Club, Dance Club, Dramatics Club, English Literary Society (ELS), Hindi Sahitya Sabha (HSS), Fine Arts Club, Photography Club, Quiz Club, Anime Society.
- **Media:** Vox Populi (Student Media Body).

### 5. Startup Incubation
- **Official Incubator:** SIDBI Innovation and Incubation Centre (SIIC), now managed under the **FIRST** (Foundation for Innovation & Research in Science & Technology) foundation.
- **Student Body:** Entrepreneurship Cell (E-Cell) – organizes the flagship "E-Summit".
- **Incubation Impact:** Nurtured over 500 startups with a collective valuation of over ₹12,000 crore.
- **Notable Supported Startups:**
    - **Phool.co:** Converts flower waste into incense and vegan leather ("Fleather").
    - **Noccarc Robotics:** Developed ICU ventilators (Noccarc V30) during the pandemic.
    - **Offgrid Energy Labs:** Developing sustainable zinc-gel battery technology.
    - **Saptkrishi:** Developed "Sabjikothi" for horticultural storage.
