### 3. Curriculum/Course Structure (Core Subjects)
- **Computer Science & Engineering (CSE):** Discrete Structures, Algorithms & Data Structures, Software Engineering, Operating Systems, Database Management Systems, Computer Networks, Compilers, Theory of Computation.
- **Electronics & Electrical Communication Engineering (ECE):** Electronic Circuits, Digital Electronic Circuits, Analog Communication, Digital Communication, Electromagnetic Waves, VLSI Engineering.
- **Electrical Engineering (EE):** Network Theory, Signals & Systems, Electrical Machines, Control Systems, Power Systems, Power Electronics, Microprocessors & Microcontrollers.
- **Mechanical Engineering (ME):** Thermodynamics, Fluid Mechanics, Kinematics & Dynamics of Machines, Solid Mechanics, Heat Transfer, Manufacturing Processes, Machine Design.

### 4. Campus Life & Infrastructure
- **Student Activities Center:** Technology Students' Gymkhana (TSG).
- **Sports Facilities:** Tata Sports Complex (featuring two football and two cricket grounds, athletic tracks), Jnan Ghosh Stadium, Aquatic Center (modernized swimming pool and water polo), Indoor Squash Court, Badminton courts, and Gymnasiums.
- **Active Clubs & Societies:** Technology Robotix Society, Pravah (Hindi Dramatics), Encore (English Dramatics), Technology Literary Society (TLS), Spectra (Fine Arts), Business Club, Quiz Club, Communique (Soft Skills), and Technology Adventure Society (TAdS).

### 5. Startup Incubation
- **Entrepreneurship Cell (E-Cell):** One of India's largest student-run non-profit organizations, established in 2006, operating under the Rajendra Mishra School of Engineering Entrepreneurship (RMSoEE).
- **Technology Business Incubator (TBI) & Facilities:** 
  - **STEP (Science and Technology Entrepreneurs' Park):** Established in 1986, providing "Wet Incubation" (labs and industrial sheds) at a 127-acre facility.
  - **TIETS (Technology Incubation and Entrepreneurship Training Society):** Focusing on "Dry Incubation" (seed funding and mentorship).

### 6. Wikipedia Cross-Verification
- **Overview:** IIT Kharagpur is recognized as an Institute of National Importance and an Institute of Eminence. It is the first IIT, established in 1951.
- **Campus Size:** 2,100 acres (8.5 km²), making it the largest IIT campus.
- **Historical Fact:** Classes initially began in the Hijli Detention Camp, a former British prison.
